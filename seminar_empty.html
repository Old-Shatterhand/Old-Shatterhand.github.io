  • Read and present one of the assigned seminar papers (assignment made at the kick-off meeting).
  • Presentations are 20 minutes with an additional 10-minute Q&A from the audience.

  • Write a 5-page report on your findings.

Presentation (30%)

Assessed on clarity, depth of understanding, and the quality of your answers to audience questions.

Notebook (30%)

  • Completeness ‚Äî does it cover all required aspects of the method?
  • Documentation ‚Äî is the code clearly commented and explained?
  • Executability ‚Äî can the notebook be run from top to bottom without errors?

Report (30%)

The report should include:

  • A short explanation of how the model or method you studied works
  • An explanation of the [technique] ‚Äî including its advantages and limitations
  • A comparison to other methods covered in the seminar or beyond
  • Your own critical reflections on this method

Participation (10%)

Active engagement during other students' presentations. Ask questions ‚Äî it counts toward your grade and improves the seminar for everyone.

Plagiarism

All submissions will be checked for plagiarism using TurnItIn. By attending this seminar, you agree that your report may be uploaded to TurnItIn for verification.

You are encouraged to use TurnItIn yourself before submitting. If plagiarism is detected, you will have the opportunity to provide an explanation. Ultimately, confirmed plagiarism will result in failing the seminar.
